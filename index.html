Freelance Services Agreement
FreelanceKit Template · freelancekit.io

1. The Parties

This Agreement is entered into as of [DATE] between Freelancer: [YOUR FULL NAME OR BUSINESS NAME], located at [YOUR CITY, STATE/PROVINCE, COUNTRY], and Client: [CLIENT FULL NAME OR COMPANY NAME], located at [CLIENT ADDRESS], represented by [CLIENT CONTACT NAME, TITLE].

2. Project Scope & Deliverables

Freelancer agrees to provide the following services:

[DESCRIBE THE PROJECT IN SPECIFIC, MEASURABLE TERMS. E.G. "Design of 5 social media templates for Instagram (1080×1080px), delivered as editable Figma files and exported JPEGs. Includes 2 rounds of revisions."]

Deliverables:

[LIST EACH DELIVERABLE: • Deliverable 1: [Description, format] • Deliverable 2: [Description, format] • Deliverable 3: [Description, format]]
Scope note: Any work, deliverables, or revisions not listed above are outside scope and will require a written Scope Change Order with additional fees.

3. Timeline

Start: [START DATE] · Final delivery: [END DATE]. Timeline assumes Client provides materials and feedback within [3] business days of each request. Delays caused by late feedback extend the deadline accordingly.

4. Fees & Payment

Total fee: [AMOUNT AND CURRENCY]

  • [50%] deposit due on signing: [AMOUNT]
  • [50%] final payment due on delivery: [AMOUNT]

Invoices due within [7] days of receipt. Late balances accrue [1.5%]/month. Work does not begin until deposit clears. Final files withheld until final payment clears.

5. Revision Policy

Includes [2] rounds of revisions. Additional rounds billed at [YOUR HOURLY RATE]/hr. Feedback must be submitted consolidated within [7] days of delivery; late feedback treated as approval.

6. Kill Fee

  • Cancelled before work begins: deposit refunded in full.
  • Cancelled after work begins but before 50% completion: deposit forfeited.
  • Cancelled after 50% completion: full fee owed.

7. Intellectual Property

Upon receipt of full payment, Freelancer assigns all rights in final approved deliverables to Client. Until then, all work remains Freelancer's property. Source files, rejected concepts, and third-party assets are excluded unless explicitly listed as deliverables. Freelancer retains portfolio rights unless Client requests otherwise in writing.

8. Confidentiality

Both parties agree to keep confidential all proprietary information shared during this engagement and not to disclose it to third parties without prior written consent.

9. Independent Contractor

Freelancer is an independent contractor, not an employee. Freelancer is responsible for their own taxes, insurance, and equipment.

10. Dispute Resolution

Disputes will first be addressed in good faith within [14] days. If unresolved, disputes will proceed to binding arbitration in [YOUR CITY, STATE/PROVINCE] before either party pursues litigation.

11. Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of [YOUR STATE/PROVINCE AND COUNTRY].

Email Sequence
3 escalating emails + 1 final handoff. Navigate between them using the tabs below. Each has a tone guide, subject line options, and annotations explaining the psychology.
Tone — Day 7:Warm and professional. Assume it was an oversight — because it probably was. No guilt trips.
Subject line options
Quick follow-up — Invoice #[NUMBER]
Friendly reminder: Invoice #[NUMBER] due [DATE]
Following up on [PROJECT NAME] invoice
Email 1 — Gentle Nudge (Day 7)
To[CLIENT EMAIL]
SubjectQuick follow-up — Invoice #[NUMBER]

Hi [CLIENT FIRST NAME],

Hope things are going well on your end.

I wanted to send a quick note — Invoice #[NUMBER] for [PROJECT NAME], totalling [AMOUNT], was due on [DUE DATE]. I know things get busy, so just wanted to make sure it didn't slip through the cracks.

I've re-attached it below for easy reference. Payment can be made via [PAYMENT METHOD / STRIPE LINK].

Let me know if you have any questions or if something's come up on your end — happy to chat.

Thanks so much,

[YOUR NAME]
[YOUR BUSINESS]
[YOUR EMAIL]
Tone — Day 14:Professional and direct. State facts and consequences. No longer casual — now you're being contractual.
Email 2 — Firm Follow-Up (Day 14)
To[CLIENT EMAIL]
SubjectSecond notice — Invoice #[NUMBER] now 14 days overdue

Hi [CLIENT FIRST NAME],

I'm following up on my previous message from [DATE OF EMAIL 1] regarding Invoice #[NUMBER] for [AMOUNT], which was due on [DUE DATE] and remains unpaid.

I'd appreciate your urgent attention on this. Please arrange payment by [DATE — 5 business days from today].

[OPTIONAL — include only if late fees are in your contract: Per our signed agreement, a late fee of [RATE] per month is now accruing on the outstanding balance. The current total is [UPDATED AMOUNT].]

If there's an issue with the invoice or something I'm not aware of, please reply so we can resolve it. If payment has already been sent, please disregard this message.

Thank you,

[YOUR NAME]
[YOUR BUSINESS]
Invoice #[NUMBER] attached
Tone — Day 30:Formal, factual, brief. No pleasantries. This reads like a letter from a business, not a person.
Email 3 — Final Notice (Day 30)
To[CLIENT EMAIL]
SubjectFinal notice — Invoice #[NUMBER] — [AMOUNT] outstanding

Dear [CLIENT FULL NAME],

This is a formal final notice regarding Invoice #[NUMBER] for [PROJECT NAME], totalling [AMOUNT + LATE FEES], now [X] days overdue.

Despite notices sent on [DATE 1] and [DATE 2], this invoice remains unpaid with no response received.

I require full payment by [DATE — 5 business days from now]. If payment is not received, I will pursue the outstanding balance through [collections agency / small claims court / formal legal process], resulting in additional costs and a potential mark on your business's credit record.

To resolve this directly, contact me at [YOUR EMAIL] or [YOUR PHONE].

Regards,

[YOUR FULL NAME]
[YOUR BUSINESS]
All prior correspondence and Invoice #[NUMBER] attached
Tone — Day 45+:Calm, almost neutral. You're not angry — you're moving on. Frame consequences around their self-interest, not your frustration.
Bonus Email — The Handoff (Day 45+)
To[CLIENT EMAIL]
SubjectInvoice #[NUMBER] — one last opportunity to resolve

Dear [CLIENT FULL NAME],

I am writing to inform you that Invoice #[NUMBER] for [AMOUNT + FEES], now [X] days overdue, will be passed to [collections agency / my solicitor / small claims court] on [DATE — 3 business days from now] if payment or a written payment arrangement is not received before that date.

I want to be straightforward: once I hand this over, the matter is out of my hands. The cost to you will increase significantly — collections fees, legal costs, and a potential mark on your business credit record are all possible outcomes.

If you'd like to settle this directly and avoid those consequences, please contact me at [YOUR EMAIL] or [YOUR PHONE] before [DEADLINE].

Regards,

[YOUR FULL NAME]

Three complete proposal scripts for different situations. Switch between them using the tabs. Each has its own subject line bank, structural annotations, and common mistakes to avoid.
You've had a discovery call. This email follows that conversation and presents your approach, scope, and investment.
Proposal Email — Warm Lead
To[CLIENT EMAIL]
SubjectProposal for [PROJECT NAME] — [YOUR NAME]

Hi [CLIENT FIRST NAME],

Really enjoyed our conversation on [DATE]. As promised, here's my proposal for [PROJECT NAME].

What I heard you need:
[1–2 sentences reflecting their core problem back in their words. E.g. "You're relaunching in Q3 and need a brand identity that can grow with you — something premium without being unapproachable."]

My approach:
[2–3 sentences on how you'd tackle this, specific to their situation. E.g. "I start with a 90-minute discovery session before any design work — it becomes the strategic foundation everything else is built on."]

What's included:

  • [Deliverable 1 — specific: what it is, format, revision rounds]
  • [Deliverable 2]
  • [Deliverable 3 if applicable]

Investment:
[AMOUNT] — [PAYMENT STRUCTURE e.g. "50% on signing, 50% on delivery"].
[OPTIONAL: "I have one spot available starting [DATE] — I can hold it for you until [5–7 days]."]

Timeline: [X weeks], starting [START DATE], final delivery by [END DATE].

If this looks good, the next step is a signed agreement and deposit — I can have the contract over same day. Any questions, happy to jump on a quick call.

Looking forward to working together,

[YOUR NAME]

Proposal Email — Cold Outreach
To[CLIENT EMAIL]
SubjectQuick thought on [COMPANY NAME]'s [SPECIFIC THING YOU NOTICED]

Hi [CLIENT FIRST NAME],

[ONE SPECIFIC OBSERVATION — proof you've actually looked at their work. E.g. "I've been following Bloom Studio's work — the way you handled the Meridian rebrand was genuinely impressive." Never: "I hope this email finds you well."]

I'm a [SPECIFIC NICHE — e.g. "brand designer for early-stage SaaS companies" not "graphic designer"]. I noticed [A SPECIFIC GAP OR OPPORTUNITY you see — e.g. "your website doesn't yet reflect the quality of work you're producing — I think that might be costing you leads."]

I've helped companies like [1–2 relevant clients] solve exactly this. I'm not pitching a full project — I'd love a 20-minute call to understand what you're working on and see if there's a fit.
